Logarithm Formula Cheat Sheet

Clean, printable log rules + key identities + quick examples. (Notation: \( \log_b(a) \) means “log base b of a”.)

Definition Laws Change of base Common log: \( \log(x)=\log_{10}(x) \) Natural log: \( \ln(x)=\log_{e}(x) \)

1) Definition

core idea
Log ↔ Exponential
Convert logs to exponent form (and back).
\( \log_{b}(a) = c \;\Leftrightarrow\; b^{c} = a \)
Conditions: \(b > 0\), \(b \ne 1\), and \(a > 0\).
Domain reminder: the argument must be positive → \( a > 0 \).

2) Log Laws

simplify fast
Product
Turn multiplication into addition.
\( \log_{b}(xy) = \log_{b}(x) + \log_{b}(y) \)
Quotient
Turn division into subtraction.
\( \log_{b}\!\left(\frac{x}{y}\right) = \log_{b}(x) - \log_{b}(y) \)
Power
Bring exponents to the front.
\( \log_{b}(x^{r}) = r \cdot \log_{b}(x) \)
Special values
Two must-know results.
\( \log_{b}(1)=0 \;\;\bullet\;\; \log_{b}(b)=1 \)

3) Change of Base

calculator-friendly
General form
Convert to any base (often ln).
\( \log_{b}(a) = \frac{\log_{c}(a)}{\log_{c}(b)} \)
Using natural log
Most common on scientific calculators.
\( \log_{b}(a) = \frac{\ln(a)}{\ln(b)} \)

4) Key Identities

undo operations
Inverse #1
Exponent + matching log cancel.
\( b^{\log_{b}(x)} = x \)
Inverse #2
Log + matching exponent cancel.
\( \log_{b}\!\left(b^{x}\right) = x \)
Tip: Always keep the base the same when canceling (same \(b\)).

5) Solving Quick Examples

patterns
Example A
Convert log to exponential form.
\( \log_{2}(x)=3 \Rightarrow 2^{3}=x \Rightarrow x=8 \)
Example B
Combine logs, then convert.
\( \log(x)+\log(2)=1 \Rightarrow \log(2x)=1 \Rightarrow 10^{1}=2x \Rightarrow x=5 \)
Always check the domain after solving (e.g., ensure \(x > 0\) where required).
